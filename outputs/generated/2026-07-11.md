## 文章摘要

本文介绍了 Meta 新推出的 AI 图像生成工具 Muse Image，该工具利用公开的 Instagram 头像生成图像但未告知用户，引发了隐私倡导者和用户的强烈反对。文章讨论了工具的功能、隐私争议、Meta的退出选项以及监管背景，最后反思了创新与隐私权之间的冲突。

## Reading Passage

Meta, the parent company of Instagram and Facebook, recently launched a new artificial intelligence feature called Muse Image. This text-to-image tool allows users to generate pictures by simply typing a few words. It is already available in the United States through the Meta AI app, web browser, WhatsApp, and Instagram Stories. However, the launch has triggered a strong backlash from privacy advocates and users alike because the tool can use public Instagram profile pictures to create AI-generated images without informing the individuals involved.

Muse Image works by taking a written prompt from a user and producing a corresponding visual. If someone wants to see a picture of a friend driving a car, for example, the AI can combine that friend’s profile picture with a driving scene. The results can look surprisingly realistic, though not always perfect. In one test, the AI placed the driver on the wrong side of the car for a British road. But beyond such minor errors, the real concern is consent. People whose photos are used do not receive any notification and may never know their likeness has been employed in someone else’s AI creation.

Donald Campbell, advocacy director at the non-profit group Foxglove, described the feature as an “obvious recipe for disaster.” He noted that social media platforms have already seen a long list of harms from non-consensual AI-altered images over the past year. The organisation Privacy International also weighed in, calling the tool “the latest sign AI companies see people’s images and data as raw material to be exploited.” One user on the social media platform X wrote that “pulling real users into generated photos without explicit consent is a privacy landmine waiting to detonate.”

Meta has responded by pointing to its opt-out settings. These controls are separate from regular account privacy options and allow public account holders to prevent their content from being reused by AI features. To activate this protection, a user must go to Instagram’s settings menu, choose “Sharing and Reuse,” and switch off the option labelled “Allow people to reuse your content on Instagram and with AI features at Meta” for both posts and reels. Importantly, the opt-out is only available to those with public accounts; private accounts are automatically excluded from the sharing system.

The controversy comes amid growing regulatory attention to AI-generated imagery. The UK media regulator Ofcom is currently investigating the social media platform X over similar non-consensual AI pictures created through its Grok tool. This broader environment of suspicion means that Meta’s move is likely to face intense scrutiny.

Meta’s blog post announcing Muse Image emphasized the tool’s advanced technical capabilities. The company explained that it uses “advanced reasoning to understand complex prompts, seamlessly blending multiple photos into high-quality creations.” Users can also choose from suggested prompts and presets to spark ideas, and they can sketch edits directly onto generated images. While the basic service is free for casual creation, heavier users may need to purchase one of Meta’s subscription plans for extra usage. The company intends to expand Muse Image to Facebook and Messenger soon, as well as make it available to advertisers.

The launch underscores a critical tension in the modern digital world: the convenience and creativity offered by AI tools often clash with fundamental privacy rights. By tapping into Instagram’s vast repository of personal images, Meta has created a product that is both compelling and unsettling. Supporters might argue that public profiles have always been accessible, and that opt-out mechanisms provide a choice. Yet the default model—where users must actively say no to having their face inserted into strangers’ AI art—reverses the traditional logic of consent. For many, this feels less like innovation and more like exploitation.

The debate over Muse Image is likely to intensify as regulators, campaigners, and the public examine the ethical boundaries of AI. Whether Meta will adjust its approach in response to the criticism remains to be seen, but the incident already serves as a powerful reminder: in the rush to build the next big AI application, companies must carefully consider who pays the price when privacy is treated as an afterthought.

## 长难句理解

> The organisation Privacy International also weighed in, calling the tool “the latest sign AI companies see people’s images and data as raw material to be exploited.”

**语法要点：**分词短语作伴随状语

**分析：**该句使用了分词短语“calling...”作伴随状语，补充说明Privacy International发表意见的内容，引导一个较长的直接引语。

**翻译：**隐私国际也表达了看法，称该工具是“人工智能公司视人们图像和数据为待剥削原材料的最新迹象”。


> Importantly, the opt-out is only available to those with public accounts; private accounts are automatically excluded from the sharing system.

**语法要点：**分号连接对比性并列句

**分析：**该句使用了分号连接两个意思相关但结构独立的句子，强调对比；同时含有形容词“available to”和介词短语“with public accounts”修饰“those”。

**翻译：**重要的是，退出选项仅适用于公开账户持有者；私人账户自动排除在该共享系统之外。


> By tapping into Instagram’s vast repository of personal images, Meta has created a product that is both compelling and unsettling.

**语法要点：**介词短语作方式状语 + 定语从句

**分析：**句首的“By tapping into...”是方式状语，说明Meta创造产品的手段；主句包含定语从句“that is both compelling and unsettling”修饰“a product”。

**翻译：**通过利用Instagram庞大的个人图像库，Meta创造了一款既引人入胜又令人不安的产品。


> The controversy comes amid growing regulatory attention to AI-generated imagery, with the UK media regulator Ofcom currently investigating the social media platform X over similar non-consensual AI pictures.

**语法要点：**介词amid引导背景 + with复合结构表伴随

**分析：**主句为“The controversy comes...”，介词“amid”引导背景情况，“with...”结构补充说明同时发生的具体事件，内含现在分词“investigating”。

**翻译：**这场争议正值监管机构对人工智能生成图像日益关注之际，英国媒体监管机构Ofcom目前正在调查社交媒体平台X处理类似未经同意的AI图片问题。

## 习语与地道表达

**a recipe for disaster** 灾难的根源，指极可能导致严重问题的方式或情况

常用于评述糟糕的决定或计划

例句：Leaving safety procedures undiscussed is a recipe for disaster.


**a landmine waiting to detonate** 一枚等待引爆的地雷，比喻隐藏着重大的潜在风险，随时可能爆发

形象化的警示用语，常与“like”搭配

例句：The lack of clear rules for user data is like a landmine waiting to detonate.


**a catalogue of harms** 一长串的伤害（或危害），指多个严重后果的罗列

正式用语，用于报告或严肃声明中

例句：The investigation revealed a catalogue of harms caused by the data breach.
