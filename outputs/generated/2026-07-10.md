## 文章摘要

本文聚焦全球三起具有代表性的公共卫生事件：英国 NHS 产科医疗服务的持续失误、高温热浪对女性造成的不平等健康影响，以及孟加拉国麻疹疫情的再次暴发。尽管三者发生在不同国家和背景下，却共同揭示了现代医疗体系在制度建设、风险防控和公平保障方面面临的挑战。文章强调，建设更加安全、包容且具有韧性的公共卫生体系，不仅需要持续投入资源，更需要透明问责、关注弱势群体，并从过往失误中不断改进。

## Reading Passage

In recent months, a series of health crises across the globe have highlighted persistent weaknesses within healthcare systems. From the maternity wards of the United Kingdom to the measles-stricken communities in Bangladesh and the rising death tolls during heatwaves, a common theme emerges: the urgent need for systemic reform, targeted awareness, and renewed investment in public health.

The United Kingdom’s National Health Service (NHS) has long been a source of national pride, offering free care at the point of delivery. Yet, in the area of maternity services, repeated failures have shattered public trust. Families who have lost mothers or babies during childbirth fear that without a fundamental cultural shift within the NHS, more tragedies will occur. BBC journalist Michael Buchanan has documented how numerous investigations and recommendations have not translated into meaningful change. The root problem, many argue, is a defensive culture that prioritizes protecting the institution’s reputation over learning from mistakes. Whistleblowers are often ignored or silenced, and staff fear speaking out. Families are calling for greater transparency, accountability, and a move toward a just culture where system-wide failures are examined rather than individuals scapegoated. Although the NHS has publicly committed to improvement, critics warn that progress is painfully slow, and lives remain at risk.

Meanwhile, as global temperatures climb, heatwaves are becoming more frequent and intense, and they are not affecting everyone equally. Health experts are raising alarms about the disproportionate impact on women. Research indicates that female physiology—including a higher percentage of body fat and hormonal fluctuations—can make it harder for women to regulate body temperature effectively. Pregnant women face even greater risks, as extreme heat has been linked to complications such as preterm birth and low birth weight. Social factors compound the danger: in many societies, women are primary caregivers and may be less able to seek cool shelter during a heatwave. They may also lack the financial resources to purchase air conditioning or take time off work. Awareness campaigns have historically been generic, but experts now emphasize the need for heat action plans that specifically address gender-specific vulnerabilities. Simple measures like establishing women-friendly cooling centers and providing targeted health advice could save lives.

Thousands of miles away, Bangladesh provides a stark example of how quickly public health progress can be undone. The country had made remarkable advances against measles, a highly contagious viral disease that can cause severe complications and death, particularly in malnourished children. Through sustained vaccination drives, cases had plummeted. However, the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted routine immunization services worldwide, and Bangladesh was no exception. As vaccination coverage fell, herd immunity weakened, allowing the virus to stage a devastating comeback. Today, hospitals are overwhelmed with more than 120,000 reported measles cases, and hundreds of children have died. Medical staff are working around the clock, but resources are stretched thin. The outbreak has exposed the fragility of health systems in low-income countries, where even temporary disruptions can lead to catastrophic outbreaks. Health authorities, with international support, are now racing to launch emergency immunization campaigns, but reaching remote and underserved communities remains a monumental challenge.

These stories, though separated by geography, share a critical lesson: health systems cannot afford to be complacent. Cultural resistance to change within institutions like the NHS, gender-blind approaches to climate adaptation, and gaps in vaccination coverage all demonstrate that equity and vigilance must be at the heart of public health policy. Addressing these issues requires not only financial investment but also a willingness to confront uncomfortable truths and engage with the communities most at risk. As one mother who lost her baby in a UK hospital said, “We need a system that learns, not one that defends itself at all costs.” That sentiment echoes across all three crises: prevention and preparedness, informed by real-world experiences, are the best defenses against future tragedies.

## 长难句理解

> The root problem, many argue, is a defensive culture that prioritizes protecting the institution’s reputation over learning from mistakes.

**语法要点：**插入语；定语从句；prioritize...over...结构

**分析：**该句包含插入语“many argue”，主句为“The root problem is a defensive culture”，后接一个由that引导的定语从句修饰culture。prioritize...over...意思是“优先考虑……而非……”。

**翻译：**许多人认为，根本问题在于一种防御性文化，它优先保护机构声誉而非从错误中学习。



> Research indicates that female physiology—including a higher percentage of body fat and hormonal fluctuations—can make it harder for women to regulate body temperature effectively.

**语法要点：**破折号用法；make it + adj. + for sb + to do结构

**分析：**破折号之间的内容是对female physiology的补充说明。make it harder for sb to do sth是固定结构，意为“使某人更难做某事”，it是形式宾语，真正的宾语是to do不定式。

**翻译：**研究表明，女性生理——包括较高的体脂率和荷尔蒙波动——使她们更难有效调节体温。



> As vaccination coverage fell, herd immunity weakened, allowing the virus to stage a devastating comeback.

**语法要点：**as引导时间状语从句；现在分词作结果状语

**分析：**As引导时间状语从句，表示“随着”。allowing是现在分词作结果状语，表示随之而来的结果。stage a comeback是习语，意为“卷土重来”。

**翻译：** 随着疫苗接种率下降，群体免疫力减弱，让病毒得以卷土重来，造成毁灭性后果。



> Addressing these issues requires not only financial investment but also a willingness to confront uncomfortable truths and engage with the communities most at risk.

**语法要点：**not only...but also结构；不定式并列；省略的定语从句

**分析：**not only...but also连接两个并列名词短语，其中a willingness后接两个不定式to confront和(to) engage。most at risk是省略了that are的定语从句，修饰communities。

**翻译：**解决这些问题不仅需要财政投入，还需要愿意面对令人不安的真相并接触风险最高的社区。

## 习语与地道表达

**shattered public trust** 严重破坏了公众信任

新闻英语中常用，描述丑闻或失败对公众信心的打击。

例句：The food safety scandal shattered public trust in the industry.



**speak out** 公开表达（反对意见）

短语动词，常指勇敢地指出问题、不公或错误。

例句：Many employees were afraid to speak out about the unsafe conditions.



**stage a comeback** 卷土重来，东山再起

原用于娱乐或体育界，现广泛用于描述衰落后重新流行或成功。

例句：After years of low sales, the brand staged a comeback with a new product line.



**stretched thin** 人手或资源紧张，不堪重负

非正式用法，形容因需求过大而难以应付。

例句：The hospital staff is stretched thin due to the flu outbreak.



**around the clock** 夜以继日，全天候

表示连续24小时不停歇地工作。

例句：Doctors worked around the clock to treat the injured.
